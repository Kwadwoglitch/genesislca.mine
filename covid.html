COVID-19 Vaccines May Have Caused Surge In Excess Deaths Since Pandemic, Says Study

COVID-19 vaccines could have been linked to a surge in unprecedented excess deaths across the United States and other Western countries since the pandemic began, a study has suggested.

According to the research that was published in the peer-reviewed journal BMJ Public Health, scientists have extensively studied mortality data from 47 countries, revealing that despite the extensive rollout of COVID-19 vaccines deaths happened at alarming levels since 2020.

According to the New York Post, the researchers of the study have also stressed the need for thorough investigations into the underlying causes of this level of mass deaths.

What does the study say about Covid Vaccines?

According to the research, the dichotomy between the intended protection against COVID-19 and the documented adverse events associated with the vaccines have been highlighted. “In the aftermath of the pandemic, the same moral should apply,” the researchers asserted, referencing the ethical imperative to safeguard lives through both containment measures and vaccination efforts.

Side effects of Covid Vaccines

According to the researchers, a few serious side effects of the vaccines that were documented in the study include ischemic strokes, acute coronary syndromes, and brain hemorrhages. “This commonality hinders clinical suspicion and consequently its detection as adverse vaccine reactions,” the study stated.

Statistics from the US Centers for Disease Control and Prevention say more than 1.1 million Americans have died from COVID-19 since the pandemic broke out in December 2019.
